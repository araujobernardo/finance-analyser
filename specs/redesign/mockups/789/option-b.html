Option B — Annotated Peaks

Same grouped bar chart as Option A, but the highest-income month gets a teal peak dot + "$X,XXX" label above its bar, and the highest-expense month gets a red peak dot + label. This draws the eye to outlier months instantly without requiring careful reading of bar heights.

Three subtle horizontal grid lines provide a y-axis reference without explicit y-axis labels. Hover tooltip still available on every bar.

Tradeoff: The peak annotations add visual noise; in months where income-peak and expense-peak coincide, the labels overlap and clutter the top of the chart.
